Learning English

发表于 2020-06-11

I’ve learned some English from “BBC Learning English” yesterday, So I want to write them down, it will make me understand them better.

The first part is Structuring answers. When we communicate with others, especially interviewing, we need to make our words understand easily, only then, will the interviewers remember you deeply. Others can not know what you want to express, when you say, “errrrr, Mmmmmm, well, errrr…I’m errr.”, it just make them feel that you are not prepare enough, you are not care about the interview. So let’s see BBC Learning English how to Structuring answers.
####1.Structuring answers####
Structure your answer!Say, firstly, then give your first reason, secondly and then a second reason. Then say, above all and give a really, really good, enthusiastic final reason!

For example.

Firstly, this job is an ideal match for my skills and experience: I’ve spent several years working in sales and I get on with people easily. Well, I mean, apart form the ones I don’t like, of course.

Secondly, I know Tip Top Trading is one of the fastest-growing companies in London, and I want to be part of that.

And above all, the reason I want this job is the work environments in Tip Top Trading. It is motivating and happy, when employees are in the right environment, they’re more energized and movtivated to achieve business and performance goals. Tip Top Trading providing a relaxing and comfortable environment, it will soothing nerves, employees will not be upset or neverous.

The second part is a news about Netherlands Chindren back to school. There are some new words for me.

####2.Back to school####
scrub-rub hard to make something clean.

shields-(here)screens used to stop infection spreading from one person to another.

visors-pieces of plastic that cover your face.

monitoring-regularly checking something.

I read the news, and I was touched by the Springboard Primary, a school in Netherlands. Although now still need to be pay attention to precautions, the teachers say, “We have some new things, new rules at the school, but the most important thing is that they’re happy and they get comfort and they can be a child”.This feels unusually relaxed. Especially compared to elsewhere in Europe - in Belgium, where the teachers are wearing visors, in Denmark where the children can’t hug their best friends. But here in the Netherlands, they believe the benefits of giving children this freedom outweigh the risks.

I am really touched, Covid-19 is dangerous, but we should not lost our love in the lock down period, we need families, we need friends, we are not alone, so we can fight and win the Covid-19 war.
